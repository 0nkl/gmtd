# The GMTD Guide

**How the system works, and how to get the most out of it.**

No technical knowledge needed. If you can send a chat message, you can run GMTD.

---

## What is GMTD?

GMTD keeps every task, idea, and commitment out of your head and in one trusted place. You talk to your AI like you'd text a sharp assistant: "remind me to call the dentist", "what should I work on?", "I finished the report". The AI files things, keeps the lists clean, and always knows the single best thing for you to do next.

It's built on a proven method called Getting Things Done® (GTD), by David Allen. You don't need to know the method. The AI runs it. You just answer questions.

## Getting started

Install the plugin (see the [README](README.md)), then start a chat and say:

> **"set up GMTD"**

You'll get a choice: a **quick start** (about 3 minutes, three questions) or a **full setup** (about 10 minutes, everything configured). Pick quick start. You can add anything later.

Both end the same way: the AI asks you to empty your head. Every task, worry, idea, and commitment you're carrying. Messy is perfect. It sorts all of it with you, and you walk away with a working system on day one.

## A day with GMTD

Here's what using it actually looks like:

**7:30 AM.** Your briefing arrives (or you ask "what's on my plate?"). Top priorities, anything due today, your meetings, and one suggested first move. Thirty seconds to read.

**10:15 AM.** Walking out of a meeting, you remember three things. You send them to your capture spot: "need to fix the invoice, ask Dana about the offsite, that book Marco mentioned". Done. Out of your head. Back to your day.

**2:00 PM.** A meeting ended early. You say: **"I have 20 minutes and low energy."** The AI answers with ONE task that fits, why it matters, and the tiny first step to get moving. No scrolling a long list. No deciding.

**4:30 PM.** "I finished the invoice fix." Marked done. If it was part of a bigger project, the next step is already queued up.

**5:30 PM.** "Wrap up." The AI notes where you left off, so tomorrow starts warm instead of cold.

**Once a week (the important one).** "Weekly review." Twenty-five guided minutes: inboxes emptied, every project checked, priorities reset. This is the habit that makes the whole system trustworthy.

## The words GMTD uses

Your tasks live in a handful of buckets. You never file anything yourself — the AI proposes, you confirm.

| Word | What it means |
|------|---------------|
| **Inbox** | Things you captured that aren't sorted yet |
| **Focus** | This week's top priorities (5 max, so it means something) |
| **Next** | Everything you could act on soon |
| **Scheduled** | Waits quietly until its date, then shows up on its own |
| **Later** | Active, but not this week |
| **Waiting On** | Someone else's move. GMTD nags them (well — reminds you to) |
| **Projects** | Anything that takes multiple steps. Each always has exactly one next action |
| **Someday** | Ideas with no commitment. Checked monthly |
| **Lists** | Books, videos, things to learn — stuff to remember, not to do |

Two more you'll see on tasks:

- **Due dates** are real deadlines ("the tax filing is due March 1"). Anything due or overdue jumps to the top of every recommendation. GMTD never invents deadlines to pressure you.
- **The 🤖 tag** means the AI can do that task itself — draft the email, do the research. Say "do it" and it does. It's GMTD's twist on GTD's famous 2-minute rule: if it's fast to do, do it now — and for an AI, a lot of things are fast.

## The five habits that make it work

The system does the heavy lifting, but it runs on five small habits:

1. **Capture everything, instantly.** The moment something lands in your head, send it — half-formed is fine. The whole point is that your head stops being the storage.
2. **Process your inbox every day or two.** Say "process my inbox." Five minutes, and every capture becomes a real, filed task.
3. **Trust the recommendation.** When you ask "what's next?", you get one answer. Do that one thing. Second-guessing the list is the old habit this replaces.
4. **Wrap up when you stop.** "Wrap up" takes a minute and makes your next session start warm.
5. **Never skip the weekly review.** Everything above stays trustworthy because once a week, everything gets checked. If you keep only one habit, keep this one.

## Extras, when you're ready

Everything here is optional. GMTD works fully with none of it.

**Connect your tools.** Connect your calendar and your briefing shows today's meetings — and the AI can block time for important tasks. Connect email and reviews catch to-dos hiding in threads. Connect Slack or Teams and you can capture from your phone anywhere. Connect your project tracker (ClickUp, monday.com, Asana, Linear…) and those tasks flow in too, so one system answers "what's next?". Just ask: "connect my calendar to GMTD."

**Pick a planning style.** How should your day start?

- **Classic** (default): priorities plus one first move; you pick tasks as gaps appear.
- **Big 3**: three must-dos. If those three happen, today was a win.
- **1-3-5**: one big thing, three medium, five small. Then the day is full.
- **Eat the Frog**: the hardest important thing first, every day, before anything else.

Say "change my daily planning style" to switch. Try one for a week; the system will even suggest a style if it notices one would fit you better.

**Focus sessions.** Say "start a focus session." One task, one goal, and the AI gets out of your way. When you come back, say "done" — progress is logged automatically.

**The dashboard.** "Show my dashboard" gives you the whole system on one screen: this week's Focus, what's due, what's waiting, every project — with filters for "I have 15 minutes" moments.

## When something feels off

**"My list is overwhelming."** Say "help me prioritize." Five minutes of triage — and if everything feels urgent, the AI sorts it into an urgent-vs-important grid that shows what actually deserves your attention.

**"The recommendations feel wrong."** The AI picks based on what you said matters most (your "Current #1"). If life changed, say so: "my #1 priority has changed." One sentence fixes every future recommendation.

**"I stopped trusting the list."** That means captures piled up unprocessed, or reviews got skipped. The fix is always the same: "weekly review." Twenty-five minutes later the system is true again.

**"I fell off for two weeks."** Nothing is lost. Say "weekly review" and the AI walks you back in — no guilt, no starting over.

**"Something in my files looks broken."** GMTD keeps automatic backups of your task file and checks its own rules constantly. Say "system check" and it will diagnose itself in plain language.

## Your data

Everything lives in plain text files in your own folder — tasks, lists, goals, settings, and a journal of what the AI did and when. You can open them, read them, even edit them. There's no server and no account. Copy the folder and your system moves with you; delete it and GMTD is gone.

## FAQ

**Do I need to know GTD?** No. The AI runs the method. If you're curious, the book *Getting Things Done* by David Allen is excellent.

**Do I need Slack or any connected tools?** No. Chat-only works fully.

**Can the AI do tasks for me?** Yes — tasks tagged 🤖 (drafting, research, writing). It asks before acting, and you confirm anything it produces.

**What if I already have a task list?** Setup offers to migrate it. Your original file is backed up first.

**Can I use it outside Claude?** Yes — the core is plain files plus one small script. Any capable AI agent can run it; point yours at [AGENTS.md](AGENTS.md).

**What does GMTD cost?** The plugin is free and open source (MIT). You need a paid Claude plan to use plugins.
