# GMTD Skill Guide — which skill does what

Plain-language overview. Any time the user asks "what can GMTD do?", "which skill is for what?", or "how does this work?" — answer from this page.

## The 5 skills

| Skill | Say things like | What it does |
|-------|----------------|--------------|
| **gmtd-setup** | "set up GMTD", "change my briefing time", "how does GMTD work?" | First-time setup interview (~10 min), migrating an existing task list, changing any setting later, and explaining the system |
| **gmtd-inbox** | "add X to my tasks", "remind me to…", "process my inbox", "do a full sweep" | Turns everything you capture — chat mentions, braindump channel, inbox file, voice memos, tracker tasks, email action items — into properly filed tasks. Asks smart questions, suggests answers, you confirm |
| **gmtd-now** | "what's next?", "I have 15 minutes, low energy", "what's on my plate today?", "show my dashboard" | Tells you the ONE best thing to work on right now; gives your daily briefing (optionally to your phone); opens the visual dashboard |
| **gmtd-done** | "I finished X", "mark X done", "wrap up" | Marks tasks complete, keeps projects moving (promotes the next step), closes your work session with a clean handoff note |
| **gmtd-review** | "weekly review", "quick priority check", "system check" | The weekly review (~20–30 min, guided), a 5-minute priority triage, and a periodic check of how well the system itself is working for you |

## A normal week with GMTD

- **Anytime:** thoughts hit your capture channel — phone, chat, wherever. Zero effort.
- **Morning:** briefing arrives (or you ask "what's on my plate?"). Top priorities, calendar, one suggested first action.
- **During the day:** "I have 20 minutes" → one recommendation. "I finished the report" → marked done, next step queued.
- **Every day or two:** "process my inbox" → captures become real tasks (5 min).
- **Weekly:** "weekly review" → inboxes emptied, projects current, priorities re-set. This is the habit that makes everything else work.
- **Monthly-ish:** the system checks itself and suggests improvements in plain language.

## Where your data lives

Plain, readable files in your own folder — `tasks.md` (tasks), `lists.md` (books/videos/ideas), `goals.md` (what matters most), `log.md` (what the system did, when), `config.md` (your settings). No server, no database, no lock-in: it's your markdown.
